• Origins

    Memphis, TN originally got its start as a trade and transport center due to its location being high enough on the Mississippi to avoid flooding. It was founded December 19, 1819.

  • The Birth of Beale

    Beale Street, as its history dictates, was originally named Beale Avenue when it came about in 1841. It was created by a man named Robertson Topp, an entrepeneur and developer, and named by him after a forgotten war hero, as the story goes.

  • Robert Church

    Church became the first black millionaire from the south after buying up property around Beale Street during Memphis's Yellow Fever Epidemic in the 1870s.

  • The Orpheum

    Around 1890, Beale Street underwent a renovation to include The Orpheum, which was then originally called "The Grand Opera House."

  • Church Park

    In 1899 Robert Church paid the city to create a park at the corner of 4th and Beale as a place where blues musicians could gather. One of the more notable things about it was its auditorium that could seat 2,000 people.

  • Overton Park and Memphis Zoo

    In 1906, Overton Park and the Memphis Zoo were established and have remained popular spots in town ever since.

  • Piggly Wiggly

    "The Piggly Wiggly, the world's first self-service grocery store, opened in Memphis in 1916." -Memphis Travel

  • Welcome Wagon

    "Memphis is the site of the first Welcome Wagon, founded here in 1928." -Memphis Travel

  • Memphis Museum of Natural History and Industrial Arts

    In 1930, the Memphis Museum of Natural History and Industrial Arts was constructed. At this time, the city's population was also 253,143. -Wikipedia Timeline of Memphis, TN

  • Graceland

    In 1948, Elvis Presley and his family moved to Memphis from Tupelo, Mississippi. In 1957, Elvis Presley purchased the property known as Graceland, which would be his home until death. Now it is a popular tourist attraction.

  • Civil Rights Museum

    "A short distance south of Beale Street, at 406 Mulberry Street, is the infamous Lorraine Motel where Martin Luther King Jr. was shot in 1968. The building is now occupied by the National Civil Rights Museum, which traces the history of civil rights movements in the US." -Planetware.com

  • Clark Tower

    In 1971, the Clark Tower was built, and the year following, the National Bank of Commerce building was constructed, as well.

  • Growing Population

    In 1980, the population recorded for Memphis had reached 646,356.

  • National Civil Rights Museum and Pyramid Arena

    In 1991, The National Civil Rights Museum was opened, utilizing the Lorraine Motel where Martin Luther King Jr. was assassinated. The Pyramid Arena, now occupied by Bass Pro Shops, also opened this year.

  • Lewis vs. Tyson, Boxing Match

    In 2002, Memphis hosted the Lennox Lewis vs. Mike Tyson boxing match in the Pyramid Arena wherein Lewis won the heavyweight title previously held by Tyson.

  • Memphis's 200 Year Anniversary

    In 2019, Memphis is now celebrating it's 200th anniversary since its establishment. It is being called, by some, "200 Years of Heart and Soul."
