• Origins

    Memphis, TN originally got its start as a trade and transport center due to its location being high enough on the Mississippi to avoid flooding.

  • The Birth of Beale

    Beale Street, as its history dictates, was originally named Beale Avenue when it came about in 1841. It was created by a man named Robertson Topp, an entrepeneur and developer, and named by him after a forgotten war hero, as the story goes.

  • Robert Church

    Church became the first black millionaire from the south after buying up property around Beale Street during Memphis's Yellow Fever Epidemic in the 1870s.

  • The Orpheum

    Around 1890, Beale Street underwent a renovation to include The Orpheum, which was then originally called "The Grand Opera House."

  • Church Park

    In 1899 Robert Church paid the city to create a park at the corner of 4th and Beale as a place where blues musicians could gather. One of the more notable things about it was its auditorium that could seat 2,000 people.
